Colour

Taken from the Friends & Co Identity Guidelines. Pantone references are print; the HEX values below are the approved digital equivalents.

The good news first. All six colours in the Identity Guidelines palette are registered in Elementor Global Colors, and every HEX value matches the guidelines exactly — not one is off by a digit. The brand and the build agree. What follows is everything beyond those six.

Brand tints · the yellow-green background

Paul's direction is to move the site off white onto this yellow-green. It reads as its own colour, but it is not a new one.

This is your lime, not a new colour. The background sits at hue 72° against lime's 73° — a single degree apart. It is PMS 2292 at roughly 7% strength. That matters for the approval conversation: "a tint of the lime already in your guidelines" is a much smaller ask than "a new brand colour".

It was previously called cream in the mockups, which caused real confusion — the earlier warm cream was #FBF6EF at hue 35°, an orange. The two donate mockups differ in nothing but this one value; the file ending -greenbg is the yellow-green direction.

Contrast checked — it holds. At 93% lightness the tint behaves almost exactly like white. Body text (#333) reads 12.08:1 against it, versus 12.63:1 on white: still WCAG AAA. Navy is 13.81:1, magenta 4.73:1, both pass AA.

One caution: blue text drops to 4.35:1. That passes for body copy because 20.8px counts as large text, but blue should not be used for anything smaller on this background.

Switching the site off white is one line. The page background is a role token — --surface in friendsco.css. It currently points at --lime-tint. Point it at --white and the whole site flips back. Nothing is hard-coded per section, so this stays a five-second decision instead of a rebuild.

What this means in practice. Five colours need a decision from Paul — green, navy, the footer band, cream and pale blue. None of them are wrong; they were chosen for good reasons and the site would look flat without them. But they exist outside the guidelines, and they are hard-coded widget by widget, so changing any one of them today means hunting through every page.

The fix is small: approve them as brand extensions, add them to the Identity Guidelines, and register them as Global Colors. After that the entire palette is editable from one screen — and the brand document finally describes the site that actually exists.

Two of them are easy wins. The yellow-green background and its hairline are tints of the lime already in the guidelines, so approving them is closer to documenting existing practice than adding anything new — and since Paul now wants that background site-wide, it is doing far too much work to stay unregistered.

Typography

One typeface: Open Sans. The Identity Guidelines list Akzidenz-Grotesk Pro Medium Condensed alongside Open Sans, but note that for external work "the Open Sans family of fonts is the preferred choice." Akzidenz is not licensed for web use and does not appear anywhere on the live site. Earlier mockups substituted Roboto Condensed for it — that substitution has been removed, which is the single biggest reason the old mockups did not match the finished pages.

The root stays at 16px. Body copy is 1.3rem (20.8px), which is what makes the site comfortable for older adults. One earlier mockup raised the root to 20px instead, which silently scaled every rem on the page by 30% and is why that page could never be matched in Elementor.

Two things worth knowing. Your Global Fonts set weights of 700 / 600 / 400 / 300 for Primary / Secondary / Text / Accent. And the "Big Statement" style at 1.6rem is not H4 — H4 is 1.5rem. They are close enough to look like a mistake when you see them side by side, but both are deliberate and both are honoured here.

Sentence case everywhere. Nothing on the site is set in all caps — not headings, not buttons, not navigation, not eyebrows. Program names keep their capitals because they are proper nouns (Coffee Talk, Cards Connect).

How the mockup set is put together

FileWhat it holds
assets/css/friendsco.cssAll design tokens, base type, layout primitives, buttons, cards. The single source of truth.
assets/css/chrome.cssHeader, navigation, footer and trust strip styles.
assets/js/chrome.jsThe header and footer markup, injected into every page so there is only one copy to maintain.
assets/css/pages/*.cssStyles unique to one page, loaded last.
_template.htmlStarter file for a new page, with the house rules written into it.

To add a page: copy _template.html, set data-page on the body to highlight the right nav item, and write the content. The header, footer, type scale and colours come along automatically — which is what makes every page in the set match, and match the finished build.
